Executive Summary: Predicting NFL Success

The Dataset & Prediction Goal

This analysis utilizes a comprehensive dataset of NFL team performance spanning from 2012 through 2024. To ensure the model remains consistent in standard team performance, we focused on 416 regular-season observations, and excluded post-season anomalies.

Our primary goal is to predict a team’s winning percentage, the metric of success in professional football. There’s initially over 140 variables in the dataset, and it was narrowed to eight strongly correlated predictors.

Why This Matters: The "So What"

Football isn't just a game; it’s a cultural and economic powerhouse. With over 100 million people tuning in to the Super Bowl annually, the ability to quantify success has massive implications:
•	Fan Engagement: Data-driven predictions fuel the conversations that keep fans invested from Week 1 to the playoffs.
•	Strategic Team Building: Front offices can use these insights to decide which players to draft or which schemes to prioritize to become true Super Bowl contenders.
•	The Betting Economy: In an era of legalized sports wagering, understanding the hidden signals behind a win percentage is invaluable for market analysis.

Our Approach & Key Findings

A variety of predictive methods were tested, ranging from simple regressions to complex machine learning algorithms. Interestingly, the simpler linear models outperformed the complex ones. Sometimes analysis doesn’t need an over-engineered solution, the simple model works just as great or even better.
Key Drivers of Victory: Through SHAP analysis (a method used to explain model transparency), we identified two dominant factors that dictate a season's outcome:
1.	Total Offensive Points: Unsurprisingly, the ability to put points on the board remains the single greatest predictor of a high win percentage.
2.	Pass Percentage Defense: Our model revealed that defensive efficiency against the pass is critical. Teams that force opponents into high-volume, desperate passing situations, often because those opponents are trailing, consistently see more wins.

While the dataset could benefit from deeper defensive metrics, the current model clearly demonstrates that efficient scoring paired with a shutdown passing defense is the most statistically sound blueprint for NFL success.
